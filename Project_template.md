## What is the problem you are solving?
The number of larger vehicles likes SUVs on the road in the United States has grown significantly over the past two decades. At the same time, pedestrian fatalities have also increased. This raises concerns about whether the rise in larger vehicles, such as SUVs, may be contributing to the increased risk for pedestrians. The problem this analysis aims to address is understanding whether the growing popularity of SUVs is associated with higher pedestrian death rates and what factors may be driving this trend.


## What tools did you use?
Python  *  Tableau  *  Google Big Query


## What insights did you or do you want to discover?/ What Solutions do you want to offer? Do People even need these solutions?
The analysis aims to discover whether the increase in SUVs on the road in the United States is related to the rise in pedestrian fatalities. Specifically, the goal is to identify trends over time, determine whether areas with higher SUV usage also experience higher pedestrian death rates, and understand whether vehicle size may contribute to more severe pedestrian injuries. These insights help clarify whether the growing popularity of SUVs may be an important factor in pedestrian safety.

If the data shows that larger vehicles contribute to higher pedestrian fatality rates, several potential solutions could be considered. These may include improving vehicle safety design, implementing stricter pedestrian safety regulations for larger vehicles, redesigning roads to better protect pedestrians, and increasing awareness about the risks associated with larger vehicles in urban environments.

These solutions are necessary because pedestrian deaths have been increasing, and identifying the factors contributing to this trend is essential for improving road safety.
